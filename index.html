Problem Statement

Aim & Objectives

To develop an efficient liquid soap formation process using potassium hydroxide saponification, analyzing optimal conditions for industrial scalability.

Chemical Theory

Saponification Process

Saponification is the chemical reaction between fats/oils and a strong alkali (KOH) to produce soap and glycerol. The reaction follows:

Fat/Oil + KOH ‚Üí Soap + Glycerol

Potassium Hydroxide Role

KOH acts as the saponifying agent, breaking ester bonds in triglycerides and forming potassium salts of fatty acids (liquid soap).

Laboratory Procedure

1

Oil Preparation

Heat vegetable oil to 60¬∞C in the oil storage tank

2

KOH Solution

Prepare 30% KOH solution in distilled water

3

Mixing Process

Slowly add KOH solution to heated oil with continuous stirring

4

Gel Formation

Allow mixture to form gel consistency (30-45 minutes)

5

Dilution

Add hot water gradually to achieve desired consistency

6

pH Adjustment

Test and adjust pH to 9-10 using citric acid if needed

Conclusion & Future Scope

Key Findings

The laboratory prototyping successfully demonstrated efficient liquid soap formation using KOH saponification. Optimal conditions were established for temperature, pH, and mixing ratios.

Future Research

Future work will focus on scaling the process, exploring alternative alkali sources, and developing automated control systems for industrial implementation.
