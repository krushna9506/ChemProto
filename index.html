Problem Statement

Traditional soap manufacturing processes lack efficiency and environmental sustainability. Our research focuses on optimizing liquid soap formation through controlled laboratory prototyping.

Aim & Objectives

To develop an efficient liquid soap formation process using sodium hydroxide saponification, analyzing optimal conditions for industrial scalability.

Chemical Theory

Saponification Process

Saponification is the chemical reaction between fats/oils and a strong alkali (NaOH) to produce soap and glycerol. The reaction follows:

Fat/Oil + NaOH ‚Üí Soap + Glycerol

Sodium Hydroxide Role

NaOH acts as the saponifying agent, breaking ester bonds in triglycerides and forming sodium salts of fatty acids (liquid soap).

Laboratory Procedure

1

Material Preparation

Measure and prepare all raw materials: Glycerol (5ml), Oil (10ml), Charcoal, NaOH (3ml), Aloe Vera Extracts (5ml), Distilled Water (20ml), and HCl (5ml)

2

NaOH Solution Preparation

Prepare sodium hydroxide solution and heat the oil and glycerol mixture to optimal temperature for initial reaction

3

Saponification Process

Add NaOH solution to the heated oil mixture with continuous stirring to initiate saponification reaction

4

Filtering Process

Filter the heated mixture using charcoal to remove impurities and achieve clarity

5

Raw Soap Formation

Allow the filtered mixture to form raw soap through natural cooling and chemical reaction completion

6

Drying Process

Dry the raw soap to remove excess moisture and achieve desired consistency

7

Final Product

Obtain the final liquid soap product with enhanced properties from aloe vera extracts

Conclusion & Future Scope

Key Findings

The laboratory prototyping successfully demonstrated efficient liquid soap formation using KOH saponification. Optimal conditions were established for temperature, pH, and mixing ratios.

Future Research

Future work will focus on scaling the process, exploring alternative alkali sources, and developing automated control systems for industrial implementation.
